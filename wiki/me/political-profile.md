# Political Profile

**One-line:** Busy Bay Area progressive who votes with his wallet and his conscience, leans on trusted sources for the small stuff, and saves energy for the races that actually matter.

## Core Identity

Lean Democrat, anti-Trump, but self-describes as "not too political." Common for the demographic — San Jose/San Mateo resident, tech-adjacent, busy. Not someone who follows politics obsessively but cares when it counts.

## Top Issues

Economy, taxes, and interest rates — pocketbook concerns, not abstract ideology. Bay Area cost of living makes this personal financial anxiety as much as politics.

## Political Style

When pushed, gravitates toward **bold progressive** — not satisfied with incremental centrism, wants someone willing to shake things up. But also practical: open to strategic voting (outcome-focused, not purity-focused).

## Local vs. National

Defers to trusted endorsements (SF Chronicle, local consensus) on down-ballot races where he doesn't have strong opinions. Engages meaningfully on races that directly affect him.
