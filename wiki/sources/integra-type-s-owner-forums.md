# Source: Acura Integra Type S — Owner Forums

Aggregated from IntegraForums threads: long-term impressions, annoyances, and first-drive reviews. Multi-owner data, medium confidence (forum/anecdotal, not controlled study).

## Pros (consensus)

- **Shifter/clutch**: universally praised. Short throws, light clutch, excellent rev-match. Called a "FWD S2000" by multiple owners.
- **Handling**: LSD pulls through corners cleanly. Chassis is stiff and predictable. Rear brake vectoring adds stability mid-corner.
- **Suspension modes**: Comfort mode genuinely comfortable; Sport+ genuinely engaging. Daily driving is smooth.
- **ELS audio**: better than Bose, owners consistently happy with it.
- **Wireless CarPlay**: seamless integration (2025+).

## Cons (consensus)

- **Interior rattles**: most common complaint across long-term owners. Dashboard, door panels, seatbelts. "Never heard so many rattles in a new car."
- **Engine sound**: turbo 4-cylinder lacks the VTEC wail of classic Hondas. "Meh" below 3,000 RPM.
- **12-gallon tank**: small for a performance car. Frustrating with 20 MPG city driving.
- **Missing features**: no memory seats, no power-folding mirrors, no rear vents (heat issue in summer).
- **OEM wheel fitment**: 19-inch wheels look sunken in wheel wells. Common fix: 5mm spacers or 18-inch swap.
- **Hill start assist**: cannot be fully disabled, annoying for manual drivers.

## Rattles — TSBs and Fixes

| Area | TSB / Fix |
|------|-----------|
| Door speaker | TSB 23-030 — plastic panel vibrating against speaker frame |
| Infotainment | Felt tape on lower clip feet (adapted from Civic TSB) |
| Seatbelt | Felt tape inside pillar trim |
| Dashboard | Varies by car — dealer-dependent |

**Dealer tip**: ask for a written plan of what they'll address before dropping off the car. Some dealers initially resist applying Civic-sourced TSBs.

## Torque Steer

Noticeable under full throttle in 2nd gear, especially on uneven/cracked pavement. Dual-axis strut suspension + mechanical LSD mitigate significantly. In normal spirited driving, most owners forget it's FWD. Purists will notice; most drivers won't care.

## Fuel Economy (Real World)

| Driving style | MPG |
|---------------|-----|
| Spirited city/mixed | ~20 |
| Normal mixed | ~24 |
| Gentle highway | ~28 |

Small 12-gallon tank = fill-up every 240 miles in city driving.
