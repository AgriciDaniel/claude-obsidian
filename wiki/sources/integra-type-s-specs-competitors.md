# Source: Acura Integra Type S — Specs & Comparisons

Data from Edmunds, USNews, CarEdge, KBB, Autoblog. High confidence on specs; medium confidence on ownership cost projections.

## Key Specs (2025 Type S)

- Engine: 2.0L VTEC Turbo, 320 hp, 310 lb-ft torque
- Transmission: 6-speed manual (only option)
- Drive: FWD, mechanical LSD, dual-axis strut suspension
- Curb weight: ~3,400 lbs
- MSRP: $52,900 base
- Cargo: 24.3 cu ft (hatchback liftback design)
- EPA: 24 MPG combined

The ITS is the only manual in class, the lightest, and the most cargo-practical by a wide margin.

## Recalls (verify on VIN)

1. **Steering gearbox** — worm gear spring manufactured incorrectly; can cause excessive steering friction. Fix: dealer replaces worm gear spring and adds grease. Free.
2. **Seat cushion frame** — 2024 models, driver seat not properly tightened. Free fix at dealer.

## Depreciation

| Timeframe | Value lost | Residual |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| 2 years (2024 model) | ~$4,861 (10%) | ~$43,500 |
| 5 years (projected) | ~$29,444 | ~$24,651 |

Type S holds value significantly better than base Integra (~27% in 2 years for base model). Limited production + enthusiast demand supports residuals.

## Ownership Costs (5-year estimate)

- Fuel: ~$2,400/yr (EPA estimate at CA prices)
- Insurance: $200–$300/month full coverage (Bay Area CA, performance classification)
- Maintenance: ~$2,346 over 5 years (Honda reliability baseline)
- Total 5-year cost of ownership: ~$43–50k (excluding financing)

## 2026 Changes

Cosmetic and tech refresh only for Type S — engine unchanged at 320hp.

Key additions for 2026:
- 9-inch touchscreen (up from 7-inch on some trims)
- Wireless CarPlay + Android Auto (was wired on 2024–2025)
- Wireless charging standard
- New colors: Solar Silver Metallic, **Urban Gray Pearl**, Double Apex Blue Pearl
- Color-matched grille on select colors

The 2025 misses wireless CarPlay and Urban Gray. The 2026 adds both, with identical performance.
