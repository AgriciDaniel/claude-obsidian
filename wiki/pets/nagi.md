## Current Medical Conditions

### 1. Chronic Lymphoplasmacytic Rhinitis (primary, ongoing)

**History:** Adopted with chronic sneezing and nasal discharge. Full mouth dental extraction Sept 2020 (severe dental disease). Referred to Sage Redwood City for CT/rhinoscopy May 2021.

**CT (May 2021):** Severe bilateral destructive rhinitis, inflammatory mineralization of right frontal sinus, bilateral chronic otitis media, possible inflammatory polyps in tympanic bullae, mild hydrocephalus.

**Nasal biopsy:** Severe diffuse lymphoplasmacytic and lesser neutrophilic rhinitis with erosion/ulceration and bony remodeling. No curative treatment — management only.

**Current symptoms:** Constant sneezing, bilateral mucopurulent nasal discharge (yellow/green). Has been stable/unchanged for years.

**Treatment history:**
- Prednisolone 5mg → 2.5mg (2021) — helped, weaned off
- Cyclosporine 25mg SID (2021) — added, then discontinued by owner 2022
- Fluticasone 220mcg inhaler (Feb 7–25, 2025) — no improvement; made discharge thicker; discontinued
- Apoquel (oclacitinib) — some benefit to rhinitis and pruritus; currently ongoing

**Current plan:** No escalation of rhinitis treatment at this time. Apoquel continues for combined rhinitis + dermatitis benefit. If flares worsen, options include: oral prednisolone course, cyclosporine retry, or CT + nasal flush.

### 2. Allergic Dermatitis / Pruritus

Skin licking, over-grooming. In 2025, had two episodes of corneal ulceration from scratching her eye (resolved with ofloxacin drops). Apoquel is primary management.

**History of Apoquel:**
- May 2025: Started 2.7mg BID — helped pruritus + sneezing; stopped briefly
- Aug 2025: Restarted 2.7mg BID → SID → back to BID Sept 2025 (corneal ulcers)
- Current: Oclacitinib 5.4mg, 1/2 tab PO q12h

### 3. Right Hip Dermal Mass (benign)

Discovered Nov 2025. 2–3mm black dermal mass. Cytology (VDx Pathology, Dr. Diana Schwartz): **compatible with benign cystic pigmented basal adnexal lesion** (apocrine ductal adenoma or trichoblastoma). Monitor only. Complete excision curative if it becomes irritating.

### 4. Heart Murmur (mild)

Grade I–II left parasternal systolic murmur noted on exam. Not clinically significant at current grade. Monitoring ongoing.
