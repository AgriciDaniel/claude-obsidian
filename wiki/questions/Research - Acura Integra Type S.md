# Research: Acura Integra Type S

Navigation: [[decisions/integra-type-s]] | [[index]]

## Overview

The Acura Integra Type S is a 320hp FWD manual hatchback sedan at ~$52,900–$54k. It is the only manual transmission option in its competitive set. Owner sentiment is strongly positive on driving dynamics and the manual gearbox, with a recurring complaint around interior rattles and fit/finish relative to price. The decision to buy is well-supported by the data. Two recalls and a known rattle issue are the main watch-outs.

---

## Key Findings

- **Manual is the differentiator.** No competitor (Audi S3, BMW M235i, AMG CLA 35) offers a manual. This alone justifies the ITS for anyone who wants a manual sports sedan. (Source: [[sources/integra-type-s-specs-competitors]])
- **Interior rattles are widespread and documented.** The #1 long-term complaint from owners. TSBs exist (door speaker TSB 23-030, infotainment felt tape fix). Dealer resolution is hit-or-miss — insist on a documented fix plan before leaving the car. (Source: [[sources/integra-type-s-owner-forums]])
- **Torque steer is present but well-managed.** Dual-axis strut suspension + mechanical LSD keep it in check during normal driving. Noticeable only under full-throttle acceleration in 2nd gear on uneven pavement. (Source: [[sources/integra-type-s-owner-forums]])
- **Two active recalls to verify on the specific VIN:**
  - Steering gearbox worm gear spring — can cause heavy steering
  - Driver seat cushion frame not properly tightened
- **Depreciation is better than standard Integra.** 2024 Type S lost ~10% in 2 years ($4,861). 5-year depreciation projected at ~$29k. Holds value due to limited production and enthusiast demand.
- **Real-world fuel economy: ~20–24 MPG.** EPA 24 MPG combined. Owners see ~20 MPG in mixed spirited driving, up to 28 MPG on gentle highway runs. Small 12-gallon tank means frequent fill-ups.
- **2026 refresh is cosmetic/tech only.** Type S engine unchanged. Key addition: wireless CarPlay/Android Auto and wireless charging now standard. If buying 2025, that's the one notable miss.
- **Urban Gray is back for 2026.** The 2026 model adds "Urban Gray Pearl" as a new color — relevant since Carbon Metallic was discontinued for 2025.

> [!key-insight] Samuel's criteria match perfectly
> Manual + 4-door + sporty + <$60k + safety tech = ITS is the only car that checks all boxes. Research confirms no serious alternative exists in 2025.

## Watch-Outs for Samuel Specifically

1. **Insurance in the Bay Area will be elevated.** Performance classification + CA metro location = expect $200–$300/month full coverage. Get quotes before finalizing.
2. **Interior rattles — get them documented at first service.** Common enough that dealers know the TSBs. Don't wait for them to worsen.
3. **Steering gearbox recall — verify on your VIN** before taking delivery. Stevens Creek Acura should handle this pre-delivery.
4. **2026 brings Urban Gray Pearl back.** If color was the main compromise on the 2025, the 2026 model addresses it — but engine/performance is identical.
5. **12-gallon tank + ~20 MPG city** means filling up often in Bay Area stop-and-go.
6. **Security plan already solid.** IGLA + OBD lock + wheel locks + Pebblebee covers the main theft vectors well given prior G35 and CTR thefts.

## Contradictions / Gaps

> [!gap] Insurance cost estimate needs a real quote
> Figures cited are national averages. Bay Area full coverage on a performance-classified vehicle with Samuel's driving history could be significantly higher or lower. Get actual quotes from multiple carriers before delivery.

> [!gap] Theft rate for ITS specifically is undocumented
> The ITS uses a push-button start and CAN-bus system that is vulnerable to relay attack (same as Civic Type R). The IGLA immobilizer addresses this — confirm with installer that it blocks relay attack scenarios, not just key cloning.
