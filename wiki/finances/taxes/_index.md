# Taxes

## Situation

- **Filing type**: W-2 (Salesforce employee) + significant stock plan activity (RSU/ESPP)
- **Complexity**: RSU and ESPP income creates cost basis calculation complexity each year
- **History**: Audited once — believed to be related to RSU/ESPP reporting
- **Current approach**: Uses a professional CPA. Does not do his own taxes.
- **State**: California (CA state income tax applies)

## Why It's Complex

RSUs vest as ordinary income (already in W-2 wages), then if held, any subsequent gain/loss is capital gain. ESPP shares are purchased at a 15% discount under Section 423(c) — disqualifying dispositions (sold within 2 years of grant) have ordinary income + capital gain components. Samuel sells his RSU and ESPP shares quickly (same or next day), so they show as short-term capital gains on 1099-B, but the W-2 already includes the ordinary income portion. The CPA adjusts cost basis accordingly to avoid double-taxation.
